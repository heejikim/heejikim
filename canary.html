Canary: Calling out greenwashing

Conscious consumerism is a movement of consumers who demand sustainability, ethicality, and transparency from companies. There is a growing demand for companies to take a political stand, which leads to consumers deciding who they will “vote” for with their money.

How might we bridge the gap between conscious consumers and more sustainable and ethical shopping?

Overview

This project was a part of professional business practice class. In a team of 4, we identified a problem space within the fashion industry, and ideated business opportunities for it.

We conceptualized Canary, which is a non-profit organization that represents conscious consumers, demanding fashion brands’ transparency, and calling out malpractices.

Problem

Greenwashing

Greenwashing refers to portraying an organization’s products, activities or policies as environmentally friendly when they are not. This practice is not only misleading, but it also distracts the industry from initiating sustainable design or circular economy initiatives.

As consumers become more conscious about their purchases and supporting different causes, companies are trying to cater to this by adapting more sustainable production chain, or initiating sustainability campaign.

Several companies have been called out, for claiming their businesses to be sustainable, without providing any information or statistics to back them up.

Solution

An anti-greenwashing campaign that involves both service design, and awareness advertisements. They will be something consumers can reference, before making purchasing decisions. The service will offer them a database where they can view a list of companies that have been accused of practicing greenwashing, and find resources for how to keep more sustainable wardrobe.

Primary Research

Insight 1

Many users have never heard of the term greenwashing.

  • 45%“I’ve never heard of greenwashing"

  • 23.3%“I’m familiar with the concept of greenwashing, and actively try to avoid brands that practice it”

  • 21.7%“I’m familiar with the concept of it, but didn’t know that it was called greenwashing“

Insight 2

Many users were positive when asked “Would learning about companies that practice greenwashing make you re-evaluate your purchasing decisions?”

Almost half of the users responded that they have never heard of the term greenwashing, and did not know that there was even such concept. Some users responded that they are familiar with the concept, just not what it was called. Surprisingly, fair amount of users responded that they are aware of greenwashing campaigns, and they actively research for the companies and avoid them.

Secondary Research

Insight 3

Consumers in general care more about who they purchase goods from.

Insight 4

Companies are aware that being green is “trending”, and some exploit that to drive more sales.

Insight 5

Generation Z and Millennials are the new spending powerhouse. 9 out of 10 Gen Z consumers believe that companies have the responsibility to address social and environmental issues.

Out of all generations, Millennials are most willing to pay more for products with better sustainability offerings. For a lot of millennials, sustainability is not something they just consider before purchases; it is a primary factor that influences their decisions.

Final Design

  • Browser Extension

    Spreading awareness regarding these deceptive practices and hold companies more accountable for their actions.

    When a user visits websites of brands that Canary categorized as greenwashing business, the Canary browser extension displays a pop-up alert, telling the user that the brand they are browsing has been accused of greenwashing. The pop-up lists examples of accusations, and leads the user to read more about the brand on the main website.

  • Greenwashing accusation database

    The alert pop-up leads users to Canary’s main website, specifically the page of the brand that displays different accusations made for them. This is a part of Canary’s database, which has stockpiled list of different brands with similar reputation.

    Brand pages are categorized into different types of accusations, so users can filter through different companies when they are searching.

    Every brand page has a section with summarized accusations made by different sources. Users can choose to read more about each on the original source.

    Integrity and trustworthiness is Canary’s principle. Every claims and accusations will be taken from credible sources, and they will be properly cited.

  • Help Another Way

    Canary acknowledges that making changes in shopping pattern may not be affordable for some consumers.

    Because of the nature of sustainable and ethical production, the alternative brands may not be affordable for many consumers. This page contains how-tos for keeping a sustainable wardrobe, without users having to make huge compromises. Most of these are small steps, that can make big changes if habitualized.

    Alternative Brands

    Canary empowers companies that are truthful to their words and advocacy.

    With the newly learned infomation about the brand’s malpractices, the user may want to look for ethical and sustainable brands to replace it. This call to action button will take users to the page with a list of such brands.

Takeaways

Designing for a social cause

I felt a strong connection to this project, because I fall under the target user base (Millennial), and I firmly stand by the cause of abolishing greenwashing.

We know that climate change and global warming are not problems we can ignore anymore, and we know what needs to be done to even get close to solving environmental problems. So we have a cause, and we know the method. All there needs to be is a design that makes problem solving more accessible - something that informs consumers, and empower them to make more “conscious” purchasing decisions. Working through project Canary made me realize just how powerful designs can be, when they successfully function as a bridge that connects users with certain motivation to what they want to achieve.
